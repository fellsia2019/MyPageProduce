Blog "Japan Garden"

Be worry

Now in the era of globalization of technological innovations, we are more and more separated from nature, its pure and free world, and more immersed in the technological world. Smartphones in our hands even when we eat and go to the toilet. Yes, through them we can receive knowledge, study what we wish, and not what the teachers require us to study at the university. Or the boss at work. Or parents who see in you their lost dreams and goals. Sometimes we are so immersed in the world of everyday routine that we forget WHO WE ARE. Technology is undoubtedly the most important part of our future and part of our evolution. But sometimes it is worthwhile to devote time to nature and the harmony of the environment. Get out into the forest, park, reserve. Breathe in “clean” oxygen. Clear your mind, your thoughts. This is really cool! You become fresher, thoughts become clearer and you understand at this moment how beautiful nature is.

Be happy

Spend more time with friends. Meet, share happiness and joy. Have more fun, go on various excursions, learn new stories of the world in which you live. Relax more actively, but do not forget about relaxation. Your body is not a pile of metal, which will endlessly keep you going, you need to keep a balance between types of recreation. While young, create with your friends such memories that in old age will evoke the most pleasant emotions on your face even on the most gloomy day. While you can, write to a friend, call him, arrange an appointment, because no one knows when your life will take the other side and you may not see each other any longer.

Blog "Relaxing on the sea"

Be Free

For a long time, dealing with our future, career, personal life, we miss the moment to relax. We are thinking - "Now I’ll build my career, arrange my future and I will go to rest where I want and when I wish." But in reality, in most cases, we arrange our careers and personal lives so much that we recover even when we have huge bruises under our eyes from 12 hours of work seven days a week, we have pain in our legs and back, when we get out of bed, there are internal problems with the body. Lack of sleep from any amount of sleep or insomnia. Mild depression, chest pain. All this happened that they worked harder than they should. We all know the proverb “Zeal and work lead to victory!”, But it is not always true. In Japan, there is even a term for doctors, 過労死 (karoshi), which means death from processing. You should not devote more time to a career than you devote yourself to rest and self-development (both spiritually and mentally). Take care of your health and be free from the world of everyday routine.
